- Course FAQs: Things You Might Want to Know（課程問題）

Below are answers to frequently asked questions about homework, exams, and grading policies.

  • Teaching Assistants (TAs)

    • - You can find the email addresses of your TAs in the Course Intro section on Cyber University.

  • Homework

    • - Homework assignments will be posted in the Announcements section on Cyber University.
    - The submission location for each homework assignment is specified in the corresponding announcement.
    - Assignments will be graded by the TA.
    - If you have questions about homework grading, please contact the TA directly.

  • Midterm and Final Exams

    • - Exams will take place in the same room where the class meets.
    - The exam schedule will follow the official university announcement unless otherwise stated.

  • Final Grades

    • - All grades (assignments, midterm, and final) will be posted on Cyber University
    - I will not respond to emails requesting additional credit unless there was a grading mistake.

    - Before You Ask for a Reference Letter（推薦信）

    Reference Letter Policy

    I am happy to support students by writing reference letters when appropriate. However, due to time constraints and the need to provide meaningful recommendations, I can only write letters for students who meet the at least one of the following criteria:
  • You have taken at least one course with me and performed exceptionally well.
  • You have worked or collaborated with me on a research project.
  • You have served as a teaching assistant (TA) for one of my courses.
  • I know you well enough to provide a strong and personalized recommendation.

    • Important Notes

  • Please do not ask for a reference letter in person — email me instead.
  • If I feel that I cannot write a strong letter for you, I will let you know honestly.
    (A weak or generic letter is not beneficial to your application.)
  • If you are unsure whether I can provide a reference, feel free to ask via email.

    • - Thinking About Joining Our Research Group?（找指導老師）

    Welcome! If you are interested in joining our research group, please read the following information before reaching out. Our approach might be very different from what you expected, and I want to ensure that you are prepared for it.
    1. We Don't Teach, We Train

      2. We don't provide standard lectures in this group. Instead, we focus on training students to become independent researchers. This means you will need to actively explore concepts, develop your own understanding, and push the boundaries of what is known. Most importantly, you will have to establish the ability to identify errors.

    2. Self-Learning Is Essential

      3. Students in our group must have both the ability and willingness to learn by themselves. If you rely on structured teaching or expect step-by-step guidance, this may not be the best fit for you.

    3. Small Group, Different Research Paths

      4. For better training, we only take a limited number of students. As a result, you will likely be working on very different topics from your peers. This means that you cannot always rely on senior students or peers for help, and you will need to navigate challenges on your own.

    4. Communication Skills Matter

      5. Research is not just about solving problems; it’s also about explaining your work clearly. Presentation skills are a key part of our training, and you will be expected to learn how to communicate your research effectively.

    5. Required Background Knowledge

      6. You must be prepared to learn certain foundational margin-topics:
      - Required: Quantum mechanics and differential geometry (you don’t need to master them before joining, but you must be willing to learn them).
      - Recommended: Basic understanding of relativity, quantum field theory, supersymmetry, and string theory.

    If you are excited by these challenges, feel free to reach out. Meanwhile, I would also suggest that you look around and see if there is a research group that suits you better before making any rushed decisions.
